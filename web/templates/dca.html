What is Dollar-Cost Averaging?

Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA) is an investment strategy where you invest a fixed amount of money at regular intervals, regardless of the asset's price. Instead of trying to time the market, you spread your purchases over time — buying more when prices are low and less when prices are high.

Example

You invest $100 every week into Bitcoin. When BTC is at $60,000, you get 0.00167 BTC. When it drops to $40,000, your $100 buys 0.0025 BTC. Over time, your average cost is lower than the average price — that's the power of DCA.

Why DCA is Extremely Powerful

01

Eliminates Timing Risk

Nobody can predict the market. DCA removes the fear of buying at the top. You buy across all price levels, reducing the impact of any single bad entry.

02

Exploits Volatility

While others panic during crashes, DCA investors accumulate more. Drops become opportunities — you automatically buy more units when prices fall.

03

Emotional Discipline

DCA removes emotion from investing. No more FOMO, no panic selling. The system runs on autopilot — set it and let it compound.

04

Proven Track Record

Historical data shows DCA into BTC over 5 years has never been unprofitable. Traditional markets (S&P 500) show similar results over 10+ year horizons.

05

Compounding Effect

Small consistent buys compound into massive positions over time. $50/week into ETH since 2020 would have turned $13,000 into over $85,000.

06

Accessible to Everyone

You don't need $10,000 to start. DCA works with $10/week, $50/month — any amount. It's the great equalizer in investing.

Important Considerations

Market Can Go to Zero

DCA doesn't protect against assets that lose all value. Only DCA into fundamentally strong assets.

Opportunity Cost

In a strong uptrend, lump sum can outperform DCA. But DCA gives peace of mind and protects against downside.

Requires Patience

DCA is a long-term strategy. Don't expect profits in weeks. The magic happens over months and years.
